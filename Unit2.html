Unit 2, Outcome 1

1.5 - Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Work is protected upon creation, meaning as soon as you post your website, you enjoy copyright protection. If you draft a website for another entity and the contract grants ownership of its elements to it, you do not enjoy copyright protection and your client can use the website material however they wish.

    • 1.6 - Issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages

    Disabled people may face barriers such as not being able to hear the audio that the website is playing, may be colour blind which makes things hard to read. Some are physical and cannot navigate the web page correctly. I have made my website more accessible for disabled people by adapting colours that are "calm" and made sure that everything has a label.

    • 1.7 - Appropriate filetypes for websites

    Files such as JPEG as appropriate because they compress the image more than a PNG which can save space on a webserver. These are common/appropriate file types: HTML - the webpage format, CSS - the styling format, MP3 - playing sounds effects on the webpage, PNG - to support transparent images and such.
